About {{ SITE_NAME }}

MetaGaP is an open platform for harmonising variant frequency data so researchers can compare cohorts with confidence.

Why we built MetaGaP

Sequencing studies often store allele frequencies, metadata, and QC measurements in disconnected spreadsheets. MetaGaP streamlines that workflow by pairing a structured database with intuitive tooling for uploading VCF files, curating metadata, and sharing results with collaborators.

What you can do

  • Import annotated VCF/BCF files and capture rich metadata alongside each sample group.
  • Search across cohorts using genomic coordinates, variant identifiers, and laboratory metadata.
  • Audit processing pipelines with full provenance of library prep, sequencing instruments, and bioinformatics steps.

Our commitments

We prioritise reproducibility, transparent data provenance, and accessibility. The interface is responsive by design, and the codebase is fully open-source so laboratories can adapt the platform to their governance requirements.

Have feedback? Reach out via the contact page—we actively incorporate suggestions from the community.

Designed for collaborative genomics

MetaGaP keeps track of each dataset’s origin, quality control metrics, and downstream analyses. Built-in validation routines protect against malformed uploads and highlight missing metadata so your team can resolve issues before downstream analyses begin.

Whether you are harmonising cohorts across institutions or building a reference atlas within your lab, MetaGaP gives you a consistent, well-documented workspace.

Upload once, explore anywhere—variant summaries, metadata, and QC metrics stay in sync across the platform.

Funding & availability

MetaGaP is maintained by an interdisciplinary team of bioinformaticians and software engineers. The project is released under an open-source license so academic groups can deploy it without licensing hurdles.

Get involved

Contributions are welcome—from UI refinements to new data ingestion pipelines. Visit the project repository to file issues, suggest enhancements, or share example datasets that would benefit the community.
